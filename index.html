Velocity power spectrum

Currently, I am developing a code that can calculate velocity power spectrum over a very large dynamical range with Dr. Hui Li.

Jan, 2022 -

wavelet scattering transform
on ism

Extracting information from stochastic field often tends to be done by a power spectrum analysis, which is very limited, or through convolutional neural networks (CNNs), which require heavy training and lack interpretability. Wavelet scattering transform (WST), an interpretable statistics inspired by CNN, which requires zero training, is recently introduced into cosmology. I use WST to study the properites of interstellar medium in simulations with Dr. Hui Li and Prof. Mark Vogelsberger. I am able to classify solenoidal/compressive modes with simple combinations of scattering coefficients.

Nov, 2021 - Nov, 2022

Alfven waves
in current sheet

With Prof. Xin Cheng, Zining Ren and Zhenwei Wang, I investigated the properties of waves in magnetic reconnection current sheets in solar flares.

I developed the pipeline to extract the wave shape from observations using Pinta label and Gaussian fitting, greatly improving the accuracy and efficiency of wave shape measurement over the previous approach to simply hand-measure slit by slit.

Nov, 2020 - Nov, 2021

Solar imaging
with CGAN

With Prof. Xin Cheng and Zhenwei Wang, I used deep learning to study relations between EUV and white light radiation of solar flares. We focused on rare solar flares with white light enhancement, known as the white light flares. I wrote an semi-automatic procedure to locate flare regions, centering the most significant white light flare. Then I trained a conditional generative adversarial network (cGAN) to perform highly realistic cross-bands image generation.
